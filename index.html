About GMA

The “General Montreal Area” is a fictional art gallery invented for the purposes of this project. Creating a great app that features artworks, as well as, artists was the main point of this project.

Project Goals

Modern art and artists are inaccessible to the younger generation who does not know much about artistic process.

1

Target Audience

To create an app for a local Art gallery to make local artists and art more accessible to millennials and to give artists a bigger platform to showcase t heir process and work.

2

Roles and Responsibilities

UX Research & Design: Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high- fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs, and determining information architecture

3

Project Duration

January 2022 - March 2022

The Main Purchasing and Artist Page Flows

Testing

After conducting an unmoderated usability study for the Art Gallery App, I was able to synthesize my findings and create an affinity diagram with all of the participants’ feedback. That affinity diagram allowed me to determine the main pain points in order to iterate on my low-fidelity prototype design.

Study Findings

1

Participants were visually attracted to bigger images and videos

2

Participants found some information should be bigger and easier to read

3

Participants found that the hamburger menu catalog should be separate from their profile

4

Participants liked the presentation of the artists' information in the app

Accessibility Considerations

1

The color palette was chosen in terms of the accessibility by basing myself of the contrast checker of webaim.org. The contrast ratio is 5.22:1.

2

The buttons and icons (calls to action) are quite big and adapted for bigger screens.

3

The app can be available in multiple languages like English and French.

4

There is a hierarchical presentation of important to least important information, which makes the app easier to navigate.

Takeaways

Impact

The feedback of my project has been largely positive:
“Including the artist information and process is something interesting I have not seen in other websites or apps.” “Having a 3D virtual visit of an app gallery is a cool immersive experience that intrigues me.”

What I learned

A wonderful first project that taught me about other people’s perspective on ecommerce and buying high-ticket items (like original art). This product is not for everyone, but having diverse opinions certainly helped my project to evolve in a better, more accessible direction.
